Glossary

  • Artificial Intelligence:AI is like a computer program that can think and learn like a human. It can solve problems, recognize patterns, and even interpret speech.
  • Authorisation: Authorization is getting permission to do something. In the context of computers and systems, it means having the rights or permissions to access certain information or perform specific actions within a system.
  • Backup:A backup is a selected copy of your computer's data, saved somewhere else so that you can recover it if something bad happens to your computer.
  • Classified: Classified means something is given a specific label or category because it needs to be kept secret. It's often used to describe information that only certain people can see, like government secrets that could be harmful if everyone knew about them.
  • Communication systems:These are methods or technology used to talk or transfer information with others, like phones, email, or messaging apps.
  • Confidential: Confidential refers to information that must be kept private between a small group of people. It's important not to share it with anyone who isn't supposed to know, like personal details in a doctor's office or private matters in a company.
  • Cyber Criminals:These are people who use computers to commit crimes, such as stealing personal information or hacking into systems. They are often reffered to as hackers or bad actors.
  • Cybersecurity:This is about protecting computers, networks, and data from being attacked or accessed by unauthorized people.
  • Data:Data is any information that's stored or processed by a computer. It can be anything from videos and photos to text and numbers.
  • Data Breaches:This happens when private information is exposed or stolen by someone who shouldn't have access to it.
  • Database:A database is like a digital filing cabinet where lots of information is stored and organized so it can be easily searched and retrieved.
  • Email:Email is a way to send messages and files over the internet. An email address is unique to each user. It stands for electronic mail and is the primary way of both identifying and communicating with users online.
  • Hardware:Hardware is the physical part of a computer or any electronic system. It's the parts you can touch, like the screen, keyboard, and mouse.
  • Identity theft:This is when someone steals your personal information, like your name and address, to pretend to be you, usually to steal money.
  • Insider Attacks: These are attacks that come from people within an organization, like employees, who might misuse their access to harm the organization. Attacks usually consist of a mixture of both insiders and outsiders.
  • Malware:Malware is software that's designed to misuse computers or networks.
  • Microsoft: Microsoft is a large company that makes computer software and video games. They're known for creating Windows, which is the system many computers use to operate, and Office, which includes programs like Word, Excel, and PowerPoint for doing schoolwork or office jobs.
  • Phishing Attacks:This is when criminals send fake messages or emails that look real, trying to trick you into doing what they ask, like giving sensitive information.
  • Privacy:Privacy means keeping something secret or private, away from other people who don't need to know about it. In terms of personal information, it's about controlling who can see or use your details, like your phone number, address, or what you do online.
  • Rights: Rights protect people and allow them to do certain things freely and safely. They are like promises that everyone should be treated fairly and have access to things like education, safety, and their privacy.
  • Scamming: Scamming involves tricking someone into giving away their money or personal details, often through deceitful offers or demands.
  • Software: Software is a program that consists of a set of instructions that tells a computer how to operate and what tasks to perform. It's what you see when you use your computer.
  • Word: Definition of word.
