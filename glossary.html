Glossary

Glossary

  • Artificial Intelligence:AI is like a computer program that can think and learn like a human. It can solve problems, recognize patterns, and even understand speech.
  • Authorisation: Authorization is like getting permission to do something. In the context of computers and systems, it means having the right or permission to access certain information or perform specific actions within a system.
  • Backup:A backup is a copy of all your computer's data, saved somewhere else so that you can recover it if something bad happens to your computer.
  • Classified: Classified means something is given a specific label or category because it needs to be kept secret. It's often used to describe information that only certain people can see, like government secrets that could be harmful if everyone knew about them.
  • Communication systems:These are methods or technology used to talk or share information with others, like phones, email, or messaging apps..
  • Confidential: Confidential refers to information that must be kept private between a small group of people. It's important not to share it with anyone who isn't supposed to know, like personal details in a doctor's office or private matters in a company.
  • Cyber Criminals:These are people who use computers to commit crimes, such as stealing personal information or hacking into systems. They are often reffered to as Hackers
  • Cybersecurity:This is about protecting computers, networks, and data from being attacked or accessed by unauthorized people.
  • Data:Data is any information that's stored or processed by a computer. It can be anything from videos and photos to text and numbers.
  • Data Breaches:This happens when private information is exposed or stolen by someone who shouldn't have access to it.
  • Database:A database is like a digital filing cabinet where lots of information is stored and organized so it can be easily searched and retrieved.
  • Email:Email is a way to send messages and files over the internet. It's like digital mail that lets you write to someone else, attach photos or documents, and send it all instantly from your computer or phone.
  • Fraud:Fraud is when someone lies or deceives others to gain something valuable, like money.
  • Hardware:Hardware is the physical part of a computer or any electronic system. It's the parts you can touch, like the screen, keyboard, and mouse.
  • Identity theft:This is when someone steals your personal information, like your name and Social Security number, to pretend to be you, usually to steal money.
  • Insider Attacks: hese are attacks that come from people within an organization, like employees, who might misuse their access to harm the organization.
  • Malware:Malware is software that's designed to harm or hack into computers and networks.
  • Microsoft: Microsoft is a large company that makes computer software and video games. They're known for creating Windows, which is the system many computers use to operate, and Office, which includes programs like Word, Excel, and PowerPoint for doing schoolwork or office jobs.
  • Phishing Attacks:This is when criminals send fake messages or emails that look real, trying to trick you into giving them your personal information.
  • Privacy:Privacy means keeping something secret or private, away from other people who don't need to know about it. In terms of personal information, it's about controlling who can see or use your details, like your phone number, address, or what you do online.
  • Rights: Rights are rules that protect people and allow them to do certain things freely and safely. They are like promises that everyone should be treated fairly and have access to things like education, safety, and their privacy.
  • Scamming: Scamming involves tricking someone into giving away their money or personal details, often through deceitful offers or demands.
  • Software: Software is a set of instructions that tells a computer how to operate and what tasks to perform. It's like the brain of the computer that you can't see.
  • Word: Definition of word.
